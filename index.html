About Us

Discover Maryland’s culinary history with our guided food tours. Learn about iconic Maryland dishes such as crab cakes, oysters, and more.

1600s: Early Colonial Cuisine

The early settlers brought English and European culinary traditions, incorporating local ingredients like oysters and corn.

1700s: Chesapeake Bay Influence

Seafood, particularly crabs and oysters, became the staple of Maryland cuisine.

1800s: Maryland’s Famous Crab Cakes

Crab cakes emerged as a signature dish, drawing from local ingredients and rich culinary traditions.

1900s: Baltimore’s Food Markets

Markets like Lexington Market became famous for offering a wide variety of fresh seafood and produce.

Present Day: A Blend of Tradition and Innovation

Maryland’s food scene now boasts a mix of traditional and contemporary flavors, from crab boils to gourmet cuisine.
